# CLAUDE.md

Marketing site for the Tesseract app (Next.js, deployed on Vercel). The
public vocabulary and copy rules live in `CONTEXT.md`; read it before
touching any text.

## Writing style: the site must read as written by a human

The site is written by a human in cooperation with AI, and it has to read
that way. Concretely:

- **No em dashes ("—") anywhere in rendered copy**, including page
  metadata, figure/SVG text, and the legal sub-pages. Use a period, a
  comma, or an occasional colon instead. In small mono labels, "·" is the
  house separator.
- Avoid AI-typical patterns: "it's not X, it's Y" chains, bold-label
  scaffolding, bullet lists where prose would do, filler words like
  "seamless", "robust", "delve", "empower".
- Vary sentence length; let some sentences be short. Plain words over
  impressive ones.
- Every claim follows the status marks and numbers policy in
  `CONTEXT.md` (shipped / evolving / planned; no unverified numbers).
