# CONTEXT — the site's ubiquitous language

Glossary for the marketing site's public vocabulary. The app repo's
`CONTEXT.md` is the engineering glossary; this file is the *public-facing*
one — the words the site teaches a first-time reader.

## The Reader

The person the copy is written for: an AI-curious Mac owner — has used
ChatGPT, owns an Apple Silicon Mac, drawn by a genuinely new kind of AI
relationship and by owning their AI. Copy is plain enough for any adult to
follow, but never dumbed down.

## The Companion

The lead of the site: a proactive entity that lives the day alongside the
user — the new category, unlike anything on the market. Contrast spine:
*a chatbot you summon* vs. *a companion that lives the day with you*.
Known internally as Jarvis; the site says "the Companion."

## The Instrument

The shipped foundation the Companion stands on: dictation, voice, the
agent/chat, and the OpenAI-compatible local server.

## Status marks

Every capability claim on the site carries exactly one status:

- **shipped** — usable today; present tense.
- **evolving** — built and usable (possibly behind a switch), actively
  being reworked; present tense + the mark. ("Refining" was considered
  and rejected as the label; the §02 Companion chip reads "evolving".)
- **planned** — design locked, not yet built; future tense, appears only
  in vision / work-ahead sections, never in feature copy.

## The paper conceit

The site is styled as an academic working paper (abstract, §, theorems,
defs, lemmas, figs). Scaffolding stays; everything inside it is plain
language. Theorem/Proof framing is reserved for claims that are genuinely
measured or checkable.

## The Filter

The Companion's noise discipline, and the site's second-most-important
idea (§01): it senses everything happening in the day and forwards almost
nothing — it interrupts only when the interruption is worth the user's
time. The hero sells trust ("tell everything"); §01 sells attention
(the only app that wants you to look away).

## Numbers policy

A number may appear in user-facing copy only if it is (a) verified against
the app repo or a real measurement and (b) meaningful to the Reader without
translation. Engineering stats live only in the developers/server block —
the one section allowed to speak jargon.

## The North Star feel (wording open)

The #353 slogan "a mind that happens to live in your Mac" is locked in
*feel* only — a mind, alive, resident in the Mac. Its wording is NOT
locked; the site may phrase it freshly. The only verbatim-locked copy on
the site: the owner's two X quotes ("AI is the signal. Everything else is
noise." · "Build it big to reach God.").
